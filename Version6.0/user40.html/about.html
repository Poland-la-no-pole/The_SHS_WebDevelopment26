About the National Hockey League (NHL)

The National Hockey League (NHL) is the premier professional ice hockey league in the world, featuring 32 teams across North America. Founded in 1917, it has grown into a global phenomenon with a rich history of legendary players, intense rivalries, and thrilling playoff action.

History

The NHL was established on November 26, 1917, in Montreal, Canada, as the National Hockey Association of Canada Ltd. It evolved through mergers and expansions, including the addition of American teams in the 1960s and 1990s.

Teams

The league is divided into two conferences: Eastern and Western. Notable teams include the Toronto Maple Leafs, Montreal Canadiens, Boston Bruins, Chicago Blackhawks, and the New York Rangers.

Championship

The Stanley Cup, awarded annually to the playoff champion, is the oldest professional sports trophy in the world, dating back to 1893.
