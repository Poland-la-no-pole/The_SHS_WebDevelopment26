History

Originally founded in 1901 as the Washington Senators, the franchise relocated to the Upper Midwest in 1961 and was renamed the Minnesota Twins to honor the Minneapolis and St. Paul metropolitan area. The team found rapid success in their new home, capturing the American League pennant in 1965 behind legendary Hall of Fame slugger Harmon Killebrew. The franchise reached its historical peak during a golden era in the late 1980s and early 1990s, winning two legendary, come-from-behind World Series titles in 1987 and 1991. Playing their home games at the beautiful, open-air Target Field in downtown Minneapolis, the Twins remain a deeply rooted cornerstone of Minnesota sports culture. Today, the club is focused on building a sustainable contender in the competitive AL Central division, utilizing an energetic mix of exciting young prospects and established roster cornerstones.

Byron Buxton

Selected second overall in the 2012 MLB Draft, Byron Buxton carried massive expectations as baseball's top overall prospect before making his highly anticipated debut with the Twins in 2015. Blessed with breathtaking, elite speed, he quickly established himself as a human highlight reel, famously winning a Gold Glove and the prestigious Platinum Glove Award in 2017 as the game's best defensive fielder. While injuries frequently challenged his momentum, his explosive "five-tool" talent remained undeniable whenever he took the field. He reached magnificent new heights by putting together a spectacular, career-best 35-home run campaign, hitting for the cycle, and earning his second All-Star nod. Now locked in as a franchise cornerstone with over 180 career home runs and 120 stolen bases, "Buck" serves as the electrifying catalyst driving the Twins' championship aspirations.
