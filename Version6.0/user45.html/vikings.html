History

Founded in 1960 as an NFL expansion franchise, the Minnesota Vikings officially began play in 1961 and quickly adopted their iconic purple and gold colors to honor the region's Nordic heritage. Led by legendary head coach Bud Grant and Hall of Fame quarterback Fran Tarkenton, the team established a dominant era in the late 1960s and 1970s, famously capturing an NFL Championship and making four Super Bowl appearances. Though the ultimate trophy eluded them, the "Purple People Eater" defense secured the team's legacy as one of the winningest and most consistent franchises in league history. Today, the modern squad plays in the state-of-the-art U.S. Bank Stadium in Minneapolis under the guidance of head coach Kevin O'Connell. The current roster is built around elite, explosive talents like superstar wide receiver Justin Jefferson and an evolving quarterback room as they push to reclaim the NFC North title.

Justin Jefferson

After winning a college national championship at LSU, Justin Jefferson was selected 22nd overall by the Minnesota Vikings in the 2020 NFL Draft. He shattered league expectations immediately, breaking the modern rookie receiving record with 1,400 yards and launching his signature "Grumman" dance into global pop-culture fame. By 2022, he completely dominated the league by pulling in 128 receptions for 1,809 yards, securing the NFL Offensive Player of the Year award as the youngest player to ever lead both categories. He has rewritten the history books by amassing 579 catches, 8,480 yards, and 42 touchdowns, making him the most productive receiver through the first five seasons of a career in NFL history. Now locked in as the highest-paid non-quarterback in the league, "Jets" remains the explosive, cornerstone superstar driving the future of the Vikings franchise.
