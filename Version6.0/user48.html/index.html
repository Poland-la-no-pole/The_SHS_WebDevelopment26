Power-Ups

From iconic mushrooms to the signature Tanooki Suit — discover every power-up that helps Mario conquer the Koopa Kingdom!

Mario's Arsenal

Super Mario 3D Land features a focused but incredibly satisfying lineup of power-ups. Rather than overwhelming the player, each ability is introduced gradually and used cleverly throughout the 16 worlds.

Most power-ups can be stored in an Item Box at the bottom of the screen and used at any time — a lifesaver in tight situations!

Super Leaf / Tanooki Suit

The star of the show. Collecting a Super Leaf transforms Mario into Tanooki Mario — complete with furry ears, a raccoon tail, and a full-body Tanooki Suit.

The tail can be used to spin-attack enemies, deflect projectiles, and most importantly — perform a slow-fall glide after jumping, letting Mario cross huge gaps.

The White Tanooki Suit (from an Invincibility Leaf) makes Mario temporarily invincible — a mercy mechanic for struggling players.

Spin Attack Slow-Fall Glide White = Invincible

Super Mushroom

The classic power-up that started it all. A Super Mushroom transforms Small Mario into Super Mario — doubling his size and allowing him to break Brick Blocks with his fist.

More importantly, being Super Mario gives you an extra hit before dying — essential for surviving trickier levels.

Every veteran Mario player knows: always grab the mushroom before you need it!

Doubles Size Break Brick Blocks Extra Hit Point

Fire Flower

The beloved Fire Flower transforms Mario into Fire Mario, letting him hurl bouncing fireballs at enemies from a distance. Fireballs defeat most regular enemies in a single hit.

Fire Mario's projectiles bounce off walls and floors, making them useful for hitting enemies around corners or across gaps.

Particularly effective against the Hammer Bros. scattered throughout castles!

Fireball Throws Bouncing Shots Ranged Combat

Boomerang Flower

New to 3D Land! The Boomerang Flower turns Mario into Boomerang Mario, arming him with the same weapon used by Boomerang Bros. enemies.

Boomerangs can be thrown and then caught on the return trip — up to three can be in the air at once. They can also collect distant coins and Star Medals!

This power-up requires more skill than the Fire Flower but rewards patient, strategic players.

Returning Projectile Triple Throw Collects Items!
