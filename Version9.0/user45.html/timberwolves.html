History

Founded as an NBA expansion franchise in 1989, the Minnesota Timberwolves brought professional basketball back to Minneapolis nearly thirty years after the Lakers' departure. The team experienced its first major golden era behind future Hall of Famer Kevin Garnett, who led them to eight consecutive playoff appearances and the 2004 Western Conference Finals. Following a lengthy rebuilding period, the franchise underwent a massive modern renaissance sparked by the explosive rise of superstar Anthony Edwards. Playing their home games at the Target Center, the "Wolves" have firmly re-established themselves as a fierce, top-tier powerhouse in the ruthless Western Conference.

Anthony Edwards

Selected first overall in the 2020 NBA Draft after a dominant freshman year at Georgia, Anthony Edwards arrived in Minnesota with explosive athleticism and a magnetic personality. He made an immediate impact, landing on the All-Rookie First Team in 2021 and rapidly evolving from a raw prospect into one of the league’s most feared perimeter scorers. Affectionately nicknamed "Ant-Man," he has completely taken over the league, earning four consecutive All-Star selections and capturing the 2026 All-Star Game MVP award. He solidified his legendary status by passing 10,000 career points, becoming the fourth-youngest player in NBA history to reach that milestone. Boasting career averages of 24.6 points, 5.2 rebounds, and 4.1 assists per game, Edwards stands as the charismatic, undisputed face of the Timberwolves franchise.
