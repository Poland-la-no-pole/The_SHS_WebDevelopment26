Wild

"State of Hockey"

History

Founded in 2000, the Minnesota Wild successfully returned NHL hockey to the passionate "State of Hockey." The franchise established themselves early with a thrilling run to the 2003 Western Conference Finals and quickly became regular playoff contenders. Today, the roster centers on dynamic superstar Kirill Kaprizov alongside a wave of exciting young talent. Competing in the tough Central Division, the current squad is focused on making a deep postseason push. Their ultimate goal remains bringing the first Stanley Cup home to Minnesota.

Kirill Kaprizov

Kirill Kaprizov was originally overlooked by many scouts before the Minnesota Wild drafted him in the fifth round, 135th overall, in 2015. He spent five dominant seasons refining his game in Russia's KHL, winning a Gagarin Cup and Olympic gold before finally making his highly anticipated move to North America. His 2021 debut lived up to the massive hype, as he set franchise rookie records and won the Calder Memorial Trophy as the NHL's top rookie. Affectionately nicknamed "Kirill the Thrill," he quickly exploded into an elite NHL superstar, famously setting a stunning franchise record with 108 points in just his second season. Now the undisputed face of the franchise, Kaprizov has already crossed the 230-goal mark and remains the dynamic centerpiece of the Wild's quest for a Stanley Cup.
